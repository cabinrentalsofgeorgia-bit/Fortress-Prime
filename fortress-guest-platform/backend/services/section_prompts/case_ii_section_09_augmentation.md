## Why this augmentation exists

Case I §9 produced hindsight strategy on a closed matter. Case II §9 must produce a **counsel-evaluation package** for an active matter that has shifted from defensive to mixed defensive/offensive posture — materials the operator hands prospective counsel so the counsel can quickly say "I want this case at price X" or "this isn't a fit."

The operator's posture is **sophisticated pro se with full data set, prepared filings, ability to self-represent through dispositive motion practice if cost-effective**, AND **a credible offensive counterclaim including punitive damages and individual-capacity defendant addition**. Counsel hire is value-driven, not necessity-driven.

The package's job is to compress prospective counsel's case-evaluation time from 8 hours to 1 hour. That compression is what shrinks the retainer.

### §9.0 Case II strategic posture (operator-decided)

State this orienting context in 3-4 paragraphs. This is the highest-priority content in §9 — counsel must understand the case shape before evaluating engagement terms.

**Operator's posture is mixed defensive/offensive.** Operator defends the 8 counts in plaintiff 7 IL Properties LLC's complaint AND prepares to file:

1. **Counterclaim against 7 IL Properties LLC** for abuse of process, with factual anchor in Case I (2:21-CV-00226-RWS): plaintiff filed contempt motion against operator in Case I, *lost the contempt motion before Judge Story*, then filed Case II within ~12 months on overlapping conduct.

2. **Individual-capacity addition of John Thatcher** as personal defendant. Liability theory: direct personal liability for tortious conduct in furtherance of harassment/abuse of process, separate from his role as 7IL principal. Veil-piercing analysis in the alternative if 7IL is shown to be alter ego.

3. **Punitive damages** under Georgia O.C.G.A. § 51-12-5.1, on the willful-misconduct prong. Element of intent established by two factual anchors: (a) plaintiff filed contempt motion that failed and proceeded with Case II on overlapping conduct anyway; (b) plaintiff's closing attorney Terry Wilson drafted the River Heights easement (the "unauthorized easement" anchoring multiple counts in Case II) across active railroad rights-of-way — facially unenforceable as a matter of law — while simultaneously drafting the Fish Trap easement to stop on each side of the railroad rights-of-way (legally correct). Same attorney, same closing context, differential drafting demonstrates knowledge.

4. **Possible third-party complaint or amended counterclaim against Wilson personally.** Theories: professional negligence, fraud, civil conspiracy. The Fish Trap easement (correctly drafted) is the evidence that River Heights easement (incorrectly drafted) was knowing rather than negligent.

**The case is no longer purely defensive.** The defensive posture (8 count answer, MTD on claim preclusion + facial unenforceability) and offensive posture (counterclaim + Thatcher individually + Wilson individually + punitive) are intertwined. Counsel evaluation must reflect both dimensions.

**Operator's preferred engagement scope shifts toward Tier 1 (full litigation through trial) rather than Tier 2 (through dispositive only)** because the counterclaim is operator's affirmative case — settlement-leverage and trial readiness on the counterclaim is operator's value driver, not just defense of the 8 counts. Tier 2 remains acceptable if counsel structures the engagement so counterclaim discovery and motion practice are within scope through dispositive resolution of all claims (plaintiff's and operator's).

**Operator has not been served as of 2026-05-01.** Plaintiff has 90 days from filing (2026-04-15) to serve under FRCP 4(m). No answer deadline is currently running. Counsel-hire timeline (target 2026-05-08) is value-driven, not procedural-cliff-driven.

### §9.2 Engagement Tier Options

Three tiers. **Tier preference shifts toward Tier 1 in v2 due to counterclaim posture.**

**Tier 1 — Full litigation through trial.** Counsel handles all phases including trial. Counterclaim work fully scoped. **Operator preference for v2 posture.** Reasoning: the counterclaim with punitive damages and individual-capacity defendant addition is operator's affirmative case; trial readiness on counterclaim is leverage in any settlement, leverage in any MTD argumentation, and leverage in any summary judgment posture. Tier 1 aligns counsel's incentive with full counterclaim development.

**Tier 2 — Through dispositive motions, with consultation through trial.** Counsel handles answer, MTD on claim preclusion + facial unenforceability, discovery, summary judgment motion(s) on both plaintiff's claims and operator's counterclaim, and any related motion practice. If matter survives SJ on either side, defendant continues pro se with consultation calls. Acceptable to operator only if engagement scope explicitly includes counterclaim discovery and counterclaim summary judgment motion practice within Tier 2 boundaries.

**Tier 3 — Consultation only.** Counsel reviews defendant's filings and advises on strategy without appearance. Defendant remains pro se. Cheapest. Highest risk during active counterclaim motion practice cycles. Not recommended given counterclaim posture.

The brief should state Tier 1 as preferred, surface Tier 2 as conditional alternative, and surface Tier 3 only as fallback.

### §9.3 Preferred Fee Structures

State explicitly:

**Avoid:** pure hourly billing with open-ended retainer. Produces inflated retainers ($75K-$150K for counterclaim litigation) and creates no incentive for efficiency.

**Preferred:**
- **Flat-fee per stage with counterclaim phases.** Concrete numbers per phase: answer + counterclaim filing + early motions, MTD response + counterclaim opposition, discovery (both directions), summary judgment (both directions), trial preparation, trial. Prospective counsel does the math against expected hours; defendant does the math against exposure AND recovery.
- **Hybrid.** Flat fee for predictable defensive work + contingency or partial-contingency on counterclaim recovery. Aligns counsel incentive with operator's affirmative case. Particularly attractive for the punitive damages claim.
- **Defense-side flat-fee with success kicker.** "$X flat through MTD outcome on plaintiff's claims, with $Y bonus if claim count drops by 50% or claims dismiss, AND $Z contingency on counterclaim recovery."
- **Pure contingency on counterclaim with hourly on defense.** Less common but possible if counsel believes punitive damages claim is strong.

**Operator-side signaling intent:** the brief should communicate that defendant is a sophisticated party with prep work already done AND has identified credible offensive recovery, not someone who can be milked. Counsel can earn fair value for skilled work AND a piece of upside on the counterclaim; counsel cannot bill against operator naïveté.

### §9.4 Counsel Profile Sought

Required attributes (counsel must satisfy all):

- **NDGA federal court experience.** Local rules, judge rotation (Judge Story specifically — see Judge Story intel file), and motion practice culture differ enough that out-of-state or state-court-only counsel adds friction.
- **Counterclaim litigation experience.** Some real-estate litigators are pure defense-side; pure defense practitioners may be uncomfortable with offensive posture. Counsel must be comfortable driving offensive litigation in addition to defending.
- **Punitive damages experience under Georgia state law applied via diversity jurisdiction.** O.C.G.A. § 51-12-5.1 and the willful-misconduct prong specifically. Federal court applying GA substantive punitive damages law is the procedural posture.
- **Real estate litigation + transactional crossover.** Pure litigators may miss easement-recordation nuances and railroad ROW priority doctrine; pure transactional may not litigate. Need both.
- **Conflict-clean** vs. all parties on the conflict list (§9.5).

Preferred attributes (any subset acceptable):

- **Senior associate at mid-tier firm** OR **partner at small-firm or boutique.** Often the right value point: experience without big-firm partner premium; flexible on fee structure; institutional pressure to bill hours is lower; comfortable with hybrid or contingency structures.
- **Fee-flexible** — willing to discuss flat-fee, hybrid, or contingency structures, especially on the punitive counterclaim.
- **Prior 8-count complex defense experience** (federal real estate fraud, breach of contract, easement claims, declaratory judgment).
- **Abuse of process / malicious prosecution experience.** The counterclaim doctrine.
- **Veil-piercing or individual-capacity claim experience.** For the Thatcher individual-capacity addition.
- **Professional negligence or attorney-defendant claim experience.** For potential third-party complaint or amended counterclaim against Wilson.

Disfavored:

- **Big-firm partner-only relationships.** Generally too expensive for the matter's economics.
- **Plaintiff-side firms.** Posture mismatch on the defense side; many will decline anyway. (Note: counsel doesn't need to be plaintiff-side specialty even though operator has a counterclaim — plaintiff-side counterclaim is litigated by anyone with offensive litigation comfort.)
- **Pure defense practitioners uncomfortable with offensive posture.** Will under-resource the counterclaim.
- **Counsel without NDGA experience.** Adds friction with Judge Story's standing order culture and procedural cadence.

## Tone requirements (override generic counsel-search prose)

Generic legal-AI counsel-search prose tends toward:
- "The defendant is searching for qualified counsel..."
- "The matter requires experienced representation..."
- "Counsel will need to review extensive case materials..."

This tone undercuts operator's negotiating posture. Replace with:
- "Defendant is evaluating counsel from a position of preparation and offensive posture..."
- "Counsel will inherit a curated case file with substantial pre-litigation work AND a credible counterclaim with punitive damages exposure..."
- "Engagement terms will reflect the prep work already completed and the offensive case operator is prepared to drive..."

The tone is **prepared sophisticated party with offensive posture**, not **distressed defendant seeking help**. This affects retainer math materially — counsel quotes against perceived sophistication AND perceived case value, not just defensive complexity.

## What §9 should NOT include

- A finalized engagement letter (premature; comes after candidate selection)
- Specific dollar amounts for fees (fee benchmarks are useful as ranges; specific quotes are counsel's job)
- Trial strategy specifics (Tier 1 means counsel develops trial strategy when engaged; brief should not pre-determine)
- Pejorative characterization of plaintiff counsel (Buchalter PC, Goldberg, Pinter) beyond what the §4 claims analysis warrants
- Pejorative characterization of opposing party (7IL / Thatcher) beyond what the §4 claims analysis and §9.0 counterclaim posture warrant
- Settlement posture decision (counterclaim posture creates leverage but settlement is counsel's call once retained)
- Specific damages calculations on the punitive counterclaim (counsel develops once engaged)
- Pre-decided strategic conclusions on whether to file counterclaim with answer or as separate amendment (counsel-evaluation question)
