Article V — Duties of Manager

Manager agrees to perform the following duties on behalf of Owner:

  1. List and market the Property on approved online travel agencies (OTAs), the Manager's direct booking website, and other distribution channels.
  2. Screen, accept, and manage all guest reservations.
  3. Collect all rental payments, security deposits, cleaning fees, and applicable taxes.
  4. Coordinate professional cleaning and property inspections between guest stays.
  5. Manage routine maintenance and emergency repairs, subject to the maintenance authorization limits set forth in Article VIII.
  6. Provide 24/7 guest communication and concierge services.
  7. Furnish monthly owner statements detailing gross revenue, deductions, and net remittance.
  8. Manage all guest reviews, damage claims, and dispute resolution.
  9. Maintain appropriate insurance coverage documentation and compliance with local regulations.

Article VII — Trust Account

All guest payments shall be deposited into a segregated trust account maintained by Manager in accordance with Georgia law (O.C.G.A. § 43-40-20). Owner funds, operating funds, escrow funds, and security deposits are maintained separately. Owner may view trust account balances, transaction history, and monthly statements at any time through the Owner Portal.

Article VIII — Maintenance & Capital Expenditures

Manager is authorized to perform or contract routine maintenance and emergency repairs without prior Owner approval for any single expenditure up to $500.00. For non-emergency expenditures exceeding this threshold, Manager shall obtain Owner's written approval prior to incurring the expense.

All owner-charged maintenance and capital expenditures are subject to a {{ capex_markup_pct }}% coordination and oversight fee, which covers Manager's vendor sourcing, scheduling, quality inspection, and project management services. This markup is disclosed on all owner statements and is included in the total charge presented to Owner.

Article IX — Insurance & Liability

Owner shall maintain, at Owner's sole expense, a comprehensive homeowner's insurance policy with a vacation rental or short-term rental endorsement, naming Cabin Rentals of Georgia, LLC as an additional insured party. The policy shall provide minimum coverage of $1,000,000 per occurrence for general liability. Owner shall provide a copy of the declarations page to Manager within thirty (30) days of execution of this Agreement and upon each renewal.

Manager shall maintain commercial general liability insurance and workers' compensation insurance as required by Georgia law. Manager shall not be liable for damage caused by guests, acts of God, or conditions pre-existing this Agreement.

Article X — Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason by providing ninety (90) days' written notice to the other Party. Upon termination:

  1. All existing reservations with check-in dates within the notice period shall be honored, and Manager shall retain its management fee for those reservations.
  2. Manager shall remit all Owner funds held in trust within thirty (30) days of the final guest checkout.
  3. Owner shall reimburse Manager for any outstanding vendor invoices incurred on Owner's behalf prior to the termination date.
  4. Manager shall provide Owner with all access codes, vendor contacts, and operational documentation for the Property.

In the event of a material breach by either Party, the non-breaching Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' written notice, provided the breaching Party fails to cure such breach within said notice period.

Article XI — Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Georgia, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall first be submitted to non-binding mediation in Fannin County, Georgia. If mediation is unsuccessful, either Party may pursue remedies in the Superior Court of Fannin County, Georgia.

Article XII — Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, warranties, commitments, and agreements between the Parties, whether written or oral. This Agreement may not be amended or modified except by a written instrument executed by both Parties.
