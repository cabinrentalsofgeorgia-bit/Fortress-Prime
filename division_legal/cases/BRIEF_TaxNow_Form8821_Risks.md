## I. WHAT IS TAXNOW?

TaxNow (taxnow.com) is a third-party platform that provides **direct, real-time access to IRS taxpayer records**. It markets itself as an "ERC Tracker" that lets businesses monitor the status of their Employee Retention Credit claims.

### How It Works

1. You register at TaxNow with your business EIN, legal name, and government photo ID
2. You verify your identity (selfie + driver's license scan)
3. You digitally sign an **Authorization & Disclosure Form** (functionally equivalent to IRS Form 8821)
4. TaxNow then pulls your **IRS transcripts** directly — showing every filing, refund, payment, notice, and adjustment on your account
5. Your **tax professional** (in this case, BizHeadLaw) gets a dedicated dashboard with your data

### What BizHeadLaw Gets If You Register

TaxNow's Tax Professional portal provides:

- **Instant IRS access** to your business tax account via Section 7216 authorization
- **Real-time transcript alerts** — push notifications when the IRS processes refunds, issues notices, or flags audits
- **Employment tax transcripts** — showing exactly when/if ERC refund checks were issued, for how much, and to what address
- **Audit detection** — early warning if the IRS initiates a review of your ERC claims
- **Up to 20% referral bonuses** for the tax professional who brings clients to the platform

### The Bottom Line

TaxNow is a **surveillance portal for BizHeadLaw to monitor your IRS account** without needing your cooperation. Once you register, they see everything the IRS sees about your business tax filings — past, present, and future.

## II. WHAT IS IRS FORM 8821?

IRS Form 8821 — **Tax Information Authorization** — is a federal form that authorizes a third party to **inspect and receive your confidential tax information** directly from the IRS.

### What It Grants

- The designated party can **call the IRS and access your account information** verbally
- They can **receive written transcripts, notices, and correspondence** about your filings
- They can see **refund amounts, processing status, adjustments, and audit activity**
- Authorization covers specific tax types and years that you designate on the form
- It gets recorded on the IRS **Centralized Authorization File (CAF)** — a permanent record

### What It Does NOT Grant

Form 8821 does **not** allow the designee to:
- Represent you before the IRS (that requires Form 2848, Power of Attorney)
- Speak on your behalf or advocate positions
- Execute waivers, consents, or closing agreements
- **Endorse or negotiate refund checks**
- Receive refunds via direct deposit
