## First-Response Path Gate

| Path | May Proceed Only If | Stop If |
|---|---|---|
| Extension request | Service/deadline is live, time is short, counsel review is incomplete, and request text is neutral. | Deadline not verified, opponent/court posture unknown, or request would waive a preserved defense. |
| Rule 12 motion | Threshold issue is materially stronger than answer-only path and supported by source/case-law memo. | Motion is being used only to buy time, would waive better issues, or lacks record support. |
| Answer only | Every paragraph response is source-reviewed, defenses are selected, and Rule 12 waiver risk is accepted. | Jurisdiction/service/venue/failure-to-state/preclusion issues have not been reviewed. |
| Answer plus counterclaims | Compulsory counterclaim risk and element proof are mapped, and offensive pleading risk is accepted. | Counterclaim theories rely on privileged, incomplete, or weakly sourced facts. |
| Counsel-filed response | Counsel conflict clears and phase-one scope/budget are acceptable. | Counsel demands unbounded restart, misses clock urgency, or conflict is unresolved. |
| Pro se emergency response | Service deadline is live and counsel is unavailable in time. | Filing is not reviewed for service, deadline, local rules, defenses, and signature/service requirements. |

## Must-Have Checks Before Any Filing

1. Save and hash the complete service packet or waiver request.
2. Verify exact response deadline from live service facts, court orders, rules, and local practice.
3. Check the live docket for proof of service, extensions, orders, amended pleadings, or plaintiff filings.
4. Decide response path: Rule 12, answer, extension, answer-plus-counterclaims, or counsel-filed response.
5. Review every admission, denial, qualified response, and lack-knowledge response against the complaint and exhibits.
6. Select affirmative defenses intentionally; do not leave known defenses out by accident.
7. Decide counterclaim posture intentionally; do not plead high-risk theories without element support.
8. Verify caption, party names, civil action number, judge initials, signature block, certificate of service, and filing method.
9. Run the pre-filing red-team review.
10. Preserve a timestamped final draft and all source support in NAS before filing.

## No-Go Rules

- No filing while service status remains unverified.
- No filing from the answer skeleton without paragraph-level review.
- No admission of mixed law/fact characterizations unless narrowed.
- No counterclaim that depends on unsanitized privileged communications.
- No Wilson-related claim without counsel review.
- No deadline computation without saving the service/waiver source.
- No local-rule assumption without checking the live N.D. Ga. source.
