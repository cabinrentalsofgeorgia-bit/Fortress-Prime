## Executive Screen

The first response should not be selected until service posture is live and the complaint/exhibits are checked against the Case I record, PSAs, amendments, inspection reports, closing file, easement instruments, deed/title records, and surveys.

Current recommended stance:

- Keep answer, Rule 12 motion, and answer-plus-counterclaim options open.
- Do not waive service, jurisdiction, venue, or Rule 12 defenses casually.
- Do not file an answer that omits viable affirmative defenses or compulsory counterclaims without deliberate review.
- Do not send privileged strategy to counsel until conflict clearance.

## Response Path Options

| Path | Best Use | Upside | Risk | Prep Required |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Answer only | If Rule 12 issues are weak or timing demands response. | Fast, preserves factual denials if drafted well. | Can waive certain Rule 12 defenses; counterclaim/defense omissions can hurt. | Complete answer matrix and affirmative-defense review. |
| Rule 12 motion first | If jurisdiction, preclusion, or failure-to-state issues are strong. | Narrows case and delays answer deadline depending on motion path. | If weak, may annoy court or educate plaintiff. | Legal memo and exhibit/docket support. |
| Answer plus counterclaims | If compulsory counterclaim risk or offensive posture is ready. | Preserves offense and leverage. | Pleading risk if facts/law not tight. | Counterclaim viability memo and evidence map. |
| Extension request | If served before counsel review is complete. | Buys time and shows professionalism. | Opponent may refuse or court may deny. | Draft concise consent request / motion. |

## No-Go Rules

- No response filing without checking service validity and deadline.
- No admission of mixed legal/factual allegations without narrowing.
- No general denial unless it is good-faith and Rule 8 compliant.
- No counterclaim unless elements, facts, and evidence basis are mapped.
- No Wilson-related claim without counsel review.
