## Counterclaim Decision Frame

Rule 13 makes compulsory-counterclaim analysis important. The operator should identify any claims arising from the same transaction or occurrence before filing a first responsive pleading. The safest operational posture is to map all potential offensive claims now, then decide with counsel or after focused legal research which claims must be asserted, may be asserted, or should be reserved.

## Compulsory Counterclaim Screen

For each candidate claim:

1. Does it exist as of the time of the responsive pleading?
2. Does it arise out of the transaction or occurrence that is the subject matter of 7IL's claims?
3. Does it require adding a party over whom the court may lack jurisdiction?
4. Is it already pending elsewhere?
5. What happens if it is not pleaded now?
6. Can it be pleaded in good faith with evidence currently available?

## Current Recommendation

- Do not file offensive claims reflexively.
- Do not omit potentially compulsory claims casually.
- Build an element-by-element memo for abuse of process first.
- Treat Thatcher individual-capacity and Wilson theories as high-leverage but high-risk until evidence is stronger.
- Keep the counterclaim workbench ready for counsel review immediately after conflict clearance.
