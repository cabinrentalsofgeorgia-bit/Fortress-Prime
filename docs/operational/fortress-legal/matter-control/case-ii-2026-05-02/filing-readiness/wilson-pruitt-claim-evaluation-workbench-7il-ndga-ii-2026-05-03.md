## Civil Conspiracy / Coordinated-Conduct Gate

Do not use civil conspiracy as a label for suspicion. It must be tied to proof of an underlying tort or actionable wrong.

| Gate | Required Showing | Evidence Type |
|---|---|---|
| Underlying tort/wrong | Identify the actual tort or actionable conduct alleged. | Fraud/misrepresentation, tortious interference, breach of fiduciary duty, professional negligence if viable, or another counsel-approved theory. |
| Two or more actors | Identify participants and their distinct roles. | Wilson Pruitt, Thatcher/7IL, opposing counsel, closing participants, or others only if source-backed. |
| Common design / mutual understanding | Show a tacit or express shared plan, not mere parallel conduct. | Email chains, timing, coordinated demands, draft exchanges, instruction records. |
| Overt acts | Tie each participant to conduct advancing the common design. | Dated source records and hashes. |
| Causation and damages | Connect the coordinated conduct to measurable harm. | Closing delay/damages, title/easement impact, litigation exposure, defense cost records. |
