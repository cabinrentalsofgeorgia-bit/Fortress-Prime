# Pre-Filing Red-Team Review - 7IL Case II

Date: 2026-05-02
Operator service status: not served as of 2026-05-02

Use this before any answer, Rule 12 motion, counterclaim, attorney package, or major counsel outreach. This is the white-shoe control layer: assume the other side will attack every weak point.

## A. Deadline and Procedure Attack

- Has service actually occurred?
- Is the response deadline calculated from the right trigger?
- Did a waiver request change timing?
- Are local rules and judge-specific procedures checked?
- Does the filing path waive any Rule 12 defense?
- Does the first response omit any compulsory counterclaim?

## B. Pleading Attack

- Are all admissions narrow and deliberate?
- Are denials fairly responsive to the substance of each allegation?
- Are lack-of-knowledge responses genuine after reasonable review?
- Are affirmative defenses fact-grounded rather than boilerplate?
- Does each counterclaim satisfy element-level pleading?
- Are legal conclusions separated from factual allegations?

## C. Evidence Attack

- What facts are stated without a source?
- Which exhibits are missing or stale?
- Which assertions depend on operator memory only?
- What will plaintiff say is contradicted by Case I?
- What will plaintiff say was waived at closing?
- What will plaintiff say is barred by the judgment?

## D. Judge-Risk Attack

- Would Judge Story view this as an attempt to relitigate Case I?
- Would the filing look disciplined and narrower than plaintiff's complaint?
- Does the filing overstate fraud, bad faith, or punitive theories?
- Does it give the court a clean reason to narrow or dismiss claims?
- Is the tone professional, restrained, and fact-first?

## E. Privilege Attack

- Does the draft reveal operator strategy unnecessarily?
- Does it disclose counsel-search thinking?
- Does it mix privileged mental impressions into public filing text?
- Are attorney communications removed or isolated?
- Are work-product notes separated from evidence exhibits?

## F. Counsel-Economics Attack

- Can counsel review this quickly?
- Is the ask scoped enough for a fixed/capped Phase 1 quote?
- Does the package show discipline rather than emotional load?
- Does it preserve operator leverage if counsel is too expensive?
