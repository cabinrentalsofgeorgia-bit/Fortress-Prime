## GNRR Crossing Agreement Source Findings

| Topic | Source Finding | Control Use |
|---|---|---|
| Railroad/GDOT status | The agreement states Georgia Northeastern Railroad Company LLC leases railroad-purpose real property in Fannin/Gilmer counties from the Georgia Department of Transportation under a lease agreement beginning July 17, 1998. | Supports the gate that crossing/right-of-way ownership and lease authority are separate from Knight/James lot ownership. |
| License grant | The agreement grants Cabin Rentals of Georgia, LLC, Gary Knight, and Lizabeth Knight an exclusive right or license to construct, use, and maintain a private road grade crossing across railroad track/right-of-way/property at Milepost 386.3. | Source supports license/right theory; does not itself prove fee ownership of the crossing area. |
| No broader right-of-way use | The agreement states that, except for the crossing itself, the licensee will not build a road or otherwise infringe on the railroad right-of-way. | Useful limit when comparing whether later easement text overreaches or conditions crossing use. |
| Private-use limits | The agreement describes crossing use as private-road access to/from adjacent land and contains controls on who may use the crossing. | Compare against 2021 and 2025 easement user classes. |
| Permit / indemnity / insurance burden | The agreement imposes permit, construction, maintenance, indemnity, and insurance obligations tied to the railroad and Georgia DOT. | Helps explain why later easement terms may need strict user and compliance controls. |
| Termination / GDOT lease dependency | The agreement states it terminates upon termination of the underlying railroad lease and includes notice language tied to Georgia DOT. | Pull underlying lease/right-of-way records before making title conclusions. |
| Property-interest limit | The agreement includes language that, except for any real property already held by recorded instrument, exercise of the agreement does not create title or property interest in the crossing beyond the license terms. | Supports a license-versus-ownership gate for counsel review. |
