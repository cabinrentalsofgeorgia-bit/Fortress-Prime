## Plaintiff's Framing

Per Complaint ¶ 11:

> "*Plaintiff brings this action based **solely on post-judgment conduct, post-closing breaches, and new, independent wrongful acts** by Defendant that were not adjudicated in the Federal Action.*"

Case II is anchored to Case I (28 U.S.C. § 1367 ancillary / supplemental jurisdiction, plus 28 U.S.C. § 1332 diversity in the alternative). Plaintiff asks the same court that entered Doc 134 (specific performance) and Doc 135 (final judgment) in Case I to now adjudicate Knight's alleged failures to comply with the post-judgment Purchase and Sale Agreements ("PSAs"), to declare void a March 17, 2025 easement Knight recorded in favor of co-defendant Thor James, and to eject Knight from a driveway encroachment at 92 Fish Trap.

The principal substantive theories:

1. **Repair-obligation breaches (Count I, supports VII).** Plaintiff alleges Knight failed to perform repair work he agreed to in the PSAs and PSA amendments that survived the specific-performance order. May 2025 inspections (Exhibits G–H) compared against April 2021 inspections (Exhibits E–F) document items "not repaired, improperly repaired, or materially worsened."
2. **Unauthorized Easement (Counts II–V centerpiece).** On March 17, 2025 — *after* the federal court's specific-performance order in Case I but *before* the June 2, 2025 closing — Knight executed and recorded an easement burdening River Heights in favor of Thor James. Plaintiff alleges (a) the easement was not prepared by a Georgia-licensed attorney, (b) it was not mutually agreed by buyer and seller, (c) it materially impairs Plaintiff's title, and (d) Thor James accepted with knowledge of the federal litigation.
3. **Driveway encroachment at 92 Fish Trap (Count VI).** Concrete driveway and turn-around serving 100 Fish Trap Road (also Knight's at the time) physically encroaches across the boundary onto 92 Fish Trap. Identified by surveyor in April 2021. Plaintiff alleges Knight did not cure or lawfully resolve before closing.
